Help found a university

Most academics join an institution. A very small number help build one.

Kremsegg University is recruiting its founding faculty — the scholars and scholar-practitioners who will shape its curriculum, set its intellectual standards, and define what this kind of education becomes.

This is an unusual invitation, and a time-limited one. The programs are in active development. The people who join now will not inherit a settled institution; they will help decide what it is.

Who we are looking for

We seek faculty who embody what they teach. Across every audience we have studied, this was the single strongest signal of credibility — scholars with verifiable academic standing who are also serious practitioners of the traditions they teach. We are building a faculty grounded in the scholar-practitioner model: people who do not only study contemplative traditions but engage them from the standpoint of lived experience, and who can hold their field in genuine dialogue with the modern sciences.

We are interested in scholars and practitioners working across, and between, areas including:

  • Buddhist philosophy and contemplative traditions
  • Contemplative science, cognitive science, and the study of mind
  • Systems thinking, organizational leadership, and systemic transformation
  • Ethics, philosophy, and the study of consciousness
  • The applied fields where this work is most needed — among them contemplative approaches to health and healing, education, conflict resolution, psychotherapy and chaplaincy, and the ethics of emerging technology and artificial intelligence

What we offer

A genuine center of contemplative inquiry rather than a conventional campus — a residential community at Kremsegg Castle in Austria where faculty, students, and visiting scholars from around the world live and learn together. Complete academic freedom under professional governance. The resources of an institution being built deliberately, with care, and for permanence. And the rare opportunity to do the most generative work of an academic life: to design, rather than to maintain.

Express your interest

Founding faculty appointments and visiting positions are being developed now. We welcome expressions of interest from established scholars and scholar-practitioners; invitations to continue the conversation will follow as positions are defined.
