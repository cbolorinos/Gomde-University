The moment is now. An innovative and outstanding university is being shaped by world thinkers in the hills of Upper Austria — designed on three core principles.

Principle One

The whole person.

Most institutions develop your mind. The Kremsegg University develops the person who uses it. Academic rigor and contemplative training are not two separate activities here — they are one. The curriculum is built around presence, interdependence, judgment, and integrity, so that what you learn and who you become advance together.

Academic Programs

Principle Two

For the world that’s coming.

This is where intelligence finds its purpose in compassion and empathy. Students learn to think systemically — to see how culture, strategy, economics, and ecology interact across scales — and to develop the inner life that makes such thinking usable under real pressure. In a world that has mastered the accumulation of knowledge but not the wisdom to apply it, this is the capability the moment demands.

Principle Three

Rooted, and open.

Founded in the Buddhist lineage of Chökyi Nyima Rinpoche and taught by faculty who are both academically credentialed and trained in the traditions they teach, the university is equally at home in a contemplative setting and a boardroom.
