A name that teaches
for a hundred years

Kremsegg Castle will hold lecture halls, meditation spaces, a library, and a living community for generations. Each can carry a name you honor.

Naming a space or an endowment at the founding is different from naming one later. These names enter the institution’s story at its origin — they will be spoken by every generation of students who studies, practices, and lives here.

Spaces at Kremsegg Castle

As the castle’s renovation proceeds through Phase II of the campaign, its principal spaces offer naming opportunities of lasting visibility and meaning:

  • Lecture halls and seminar rooms — the rooms where rigorous study happens daily.
  • Meditation halls — the contemplative heart of the campus, in use every day of the academic year.
  • The Penzo Library and contemplative archives — holding both Western scholarship and the treasures of the contemplative traditions.
  • Residential wings and common rooms — where the living community of faculty and students takes shape.
  • Gardens and parkland features — within the forty-two hectares of grounds that support study and reflection.

Endowed positions and funds

  • Endowed chairs and professorships — attaching a name to the scholars who define the university’s intellectual life, in fields from Buddhist philosophy to contemplative science and coherent leadership.
  • Named scholarship funds — ensuring this education reaches students of every background, in perpetuity.
  • Program and initiative endowments — sustaining summer intensives, research programs, or the Kremsegg Cultural Programs.

A complete inventory of naming opportunities, with associated gift levels, is shared with prospective founding partners in conversation. Every naming is approached individually, with care for both the donor’s intention and the dignity of the institution.
